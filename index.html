iThe Plan

Why this works and what to grab before you start.

The one idea that makes this work

Samosas and kachori use a two-stage fry: a long, slow fry at 275°F sets a pale, blistered shell, then a quick fry at 350°F finishes them deep golden. The slow fry can happen before you leave; the 2–3 minute finish happens when you get home. That split is your airport break — par-fry early, finish to order, serve hot. Jeera rice and bhindi get the same treatment: bulk of the work ahead, fast finish on return.

✓ Already sorted

Okra, frying oil, and 5 lb potatoes boiled & chilled (the big samosa make-ahead). Plus basmati, ghee, frozen peas, ajwain, amchur, coriander & garam masala, chickpea flour, chilies powders, onions, tortillas.

● Still to grab

Fresh cilantro (samosa filling + bhindi & kachori garnish) and, if you want them, a few fresh green chilies. Everything else is in the larder.

Do this first

Wash the okra and spread it on a towel to dry now — it needs to be bone dry before slicing or the bhindi turns slimy. It can air-dry while you make the dough.

1Master Khasta Dough

One stiff, ghee-rich dough feeds both the samosas and the kachori. Make it as a single batch and split it.

Makes: ~40–48 samosas + ~15 kachori Rest: 45–60 min
  • 12 cups all-purpose flour (1440g)
  • 24 tbsp ghee (1½ cups), room temp
  • 4½ tsp fine salt
  • 3½ tsp ajwain (carom) seeds
  • ~3¼ cups cold water
  1. In a large bowl, stir together the 12 cups flour, 4½ tsp salt and 3½ tsp ajwain.
  2. Add the 24 tbsp ghee and rub it in with both hands 3–4 min until the flour looks like coarse sand and clumps when squeezed.
  3. Add the ~3¼ cups cold water gradually, mixing into a firm, stiff, non-sticky dough — stop the moment it comes together. Knead 4 min until smooth.
  4. Cover and rest 45–60 min. Then split: about ¾ for samosas, ¼ for kachori.

Why stiff

Flakiness comes from a stiff, low-hydration dough plus the slow fry — not from extra fat. A wet or greasy dough won't seal and reads as oily. If you'd rather make them separately: samosa portion is 9 cups flour / 18 tbsp ghee; kachori portion is 3 cups flour / 6 tbsp ghee.

2North Indian Halwai Samosas

Flaky, blistered chaat-shop potato samosas. Potatoes are already boiled and chilled — that's the single biggest texture win, done.

Yields: ~40–48 Filling: ~30 min Fry: two-stage, see step 8
  • ¾ of the master dough
  • 5 lb potatoes, boiled, chilled, peeled
  • 6 tbsp ghee (filling)
  • 7 tsp cumin seeds (3 roast in blend · 2 bhuna jeera · 2 temper)
  • 4 tbsp coriander seeds, dry-roasted
  • 3 tsp fennel seeds, dry-roasted
  • ¼ tsp asafoetida (hing)
  • ¼ cup minced fresh ginger
  • 6 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped (optional — omit for purist halwai)
  • 2 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 tsp hot red chili powder (lal mirch, not Kashmiri)
  • ½ cup raw cashews, crushed
  • 1 tbsp amchur
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp kasuri methi (optional)
  • 1 cup cilantro, chopped
  • 1 lemon, juice (reserved)
  • ~2½ tsp salt + ¾ tsp black salt
  • 3 tbsp flour + 3 tbsp water (seal paste)
  • Neutral oil, 3 inches deep, to fry
  1. SpicesDry-roast 4 tbsp coriander seeds, 3 tsp fennel seeds and 3 tsp cumin seeds ~3 min until fragrant. Coarsely crush. Separately roast and finely grind 2 tsp cumin as bhuna jeera — reserve it for step 4. (The last 2 tsp cumin is for tempering in step 3.)
  2. PrepCrumble the chilled potatoes into ½-inch chunks (don't mash). Mince ¼ cup ginger, chop 6 green chilies and 2 onions, crush ½ cup cashews, chop 1 cup cilantro.
  3. Cook fillingHeat 6 tbsp ghee; add 2 tsp cumin seeds, 30 sec, then ¼ tsp hing, the ginger and chilies, 1 min. Add the onions, cook 6–8 min to drive off moisture. Lower heat; add 2 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp Kashmiri + 1 tsp hot (lal mirch) chili and the crushed seed blend; toast 60 sec.
  4. Fold in the potatoes, ½ cup cashews, ~2½ tsp salt and ¾ tsp black salt, 4 min. Add 1 tbsp amchur, 2 tsp garam masala and 1 tbsp kasuri methi, 1–2 min. Off heat stir in 1 cup cilantro, the reserved bhuna jeera, and the juice of 1 lemon. Taste — punchy, tangy, sulfur note from the black salt. Cool completely.
  5. Seal glueMix 3 tbsp flour with 3 tbsp water into a thick paste — it grips far better than plain water.
  6. ShapeRoll a dough ball into a 6–7" oval, ~2mm thick; cut in half. Brush the straight edge with paste, fold into a cone overlapping ¼", press the seam. Fill with 2–3 tbsp filling, brush the rim, pinch closed with no gaps. Stand upright under a damp towel.
  7. HoldFor tonight: keep one tray to fry; freeze the rest shaped & raw on a tray, then bag. Fry from frozen later, adding 3–4 min to the 275°F slow fry.
  8. Two-stage fryNow (before JFK): heat oil to 275°F and fry tonight's batch low and slow 18–22 min — barely bubbling, crust pale and blistered. Cool on a rack. Later (back home): raise oil to 350°F and finish 2–3 min to deep golden.

Notes

  • Cooking the onions down is what keeps the filling dry so the seal doesn't steam open.
  • Don't second-fry all 48 — finish only what you'll eat; the freezer batch is next week's dinner.
  • Council: Jaffrey-approved — ajwain crust, dry-roasted whole spices, hing, bhuna jeera, kasuri methi, two-stage khasta fry. Finishing acid (amchur + lemon) locked.

3Matar ki Kachori

Flaky fried pastry stuffed with a dry, spiced crushed-pea filling. Same dough, same fry as the samosas — they batch together.

Yields: ~15 Filling: ~20 min Fry: two-stage
  • ¼ of the master dough
  • 2 cups green peas, thawed, coarsely crushed
  • 2 tbsp ghee (filling)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • 2 green chilies, minced
  • 2 tbsp besan (chickpea flour)
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp fennel seeds, ground
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • ½ tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • ¾ tsp salt
  • ¾ tsp amchur
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ¼ tsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp cilantro, chopped
  • Neutral oil, 3 inches deep, to fry
  1. FillingHeat 2 tbsp ghee; add 1 tsp cumin seeds + 1 pinch hing, 30 sec. Add 1 tbsp ginger and 2 minced chilies, 1 min.
  2. Add 2 tbsp besan and toast 2 min until nutty — this binds the filling and soaks up pea moisture so the kachori won't burst.
  3. Add the 2 cups crushed peas, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp ground fennel, ½ tsp cumin, ½ tsp Kashmiri chili and ¾ tsp salt. Cook on medium 8–10 min until dry and holding together.
  4. Off heat, stir in ¾ tsp amchur, ½ tsp garam masala, ¼ tsp sugar and 2 tbsp cilantro. Cool completely.
  5. ShapeDivide the dough into ~15 balls. Cup each into a 3" disc thicker at the center, fill with ~1 tbsp filling, pleat closed, and gently flatten to a 2½–3" disc with no cracks.
  6. Two-stage frySame as the samosas: 275°F low fry 12–15 min until pale and puffed, then 350°F for 2–3 min to deep golden. Par-fry a few tonight alongside the samosas; refrigerate or freeze the rest.

Make it "later" or "some tonight"

Filling keeps 3–4 days refrigerated; shaped raw kachori refrigerate 1–2 days or freeze. For a taste tonight, par-fry 3–4 with the samosas and finish them in the same 350°F pass when you get home.

4Jeera Rice

Rice cooker does the work; a finishing tadka gives it the toasted-cumin perfume. Your variation adds garam masala + black pepper.

Serves: 4 Active: ~5 min
  • 1½ cups basmati rice
  • 2¼ cups water
  • 1 tsp salt + more to taste
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tbsp ghee (tadka)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds (tadka)
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ¼ tsp finely ground black pepper
  1. Rinse 1½ cups basmati until the water runs mostly clear. Add to the cooker with 2¼ cups water, 1 tsp salt and 1 bay leaf. Start it; leave on warm when done.
  2. Finish on returnMelt 2 tbsp ghee, add 1 tsp cumin seeds, sizzle 30–60 sec. Off heat stir in ½ tsp garam masala + ¼ tsp black pepper. Pour over the rice, pull the bay leaf, fluff. Taste and add ¼–½ tsp salt as needed.

5Dry-Fried Bhindi

Okra dry-fried uncovered until the slime's gone and the edges crisp, finished with garam masala off the heat. The one okra dish that survives a hold.

Serves: 2–3 Cook: ~25 min
  • 1 lb okra, dried, ½" rounds
  • 3 tbsp neutral oil (or 2 tbsp neutral + 1 tbsp mustard oil)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 pinch asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 medium yellow onion, thinly sliced (optional)
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ¾ tsp Kashmiri red chili powder
  • ¾ tsp amchur
  • ¾ tsp garam masala
  • ¾ tsp salt (added late)
  • ½ lemon, juice
  • 2 tbsp cilantro, chopped
  • 1 pinch chaat masala (optional)
  1. Prep aheadWash 1 lb okra and dry it completely (towel, 1–2 hrs). Trim caps, slice into ½" rounds. Wet okra is the only reason bhindi goes slimy.
  2. Heat 3 tbsp oil over medium-high until shimmering. Add 1 tsp cumin seeds + 1 pinch hing, 30 sec.
  3. Add the okra in a near-single layer. Don't stir 3–4 min — let it sear. Then stir occasionally, uncovered, 12–15 min until the slime's gone and the edges brown. Never cover.
  4. Push aside; add 1 sliced onion + 2 slit chilies, 3–4 min.
  5. Lower to medium; add ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp coriander, ¾ tsp Kashmiri chili, toss 60 sec.
  6. Add ¾ tsp salt (now, so it doesn't draw moisture) and ¾ tsp amchur, 1–2 min.
  7. Off heat: ¾ tsp garam masala, juice of ½ lemon, 2 tbsp cilantro (+ optional pinch chaat masala). Taste — tangy, toasty, crisp-edged.

Tonight's hold

Cook through step 5 (the spice toast) before you leave, slightly underdone. On return, re-crisp in a screaming-hot pan 4–5 min, then do step 6 (¾ tsp salt + ¾ tsp amchur) and step 7 (off-heat ¾ tsp garam masala, lemon, cilantro) fresh. Because it's dry, it re-crisps instead of going mushy.

6Cowboy Colada · Ninja Slushi

The welcome-home drink. Start it before you leave; it freezes in 15–60 min and holds slush for hours.

The mix

Your cowboy colada — coconut cream + pineapple base, spiked to taste. Keep the total under ~2.5% ABV so it actually freezes (rough guide: no more than ~½ cup spirit per full pitcher of mix).

  1. Load and start the Slushi ~8:30, before the airport run. It freezes in 15–60 min and holds slush for hours, so it'll be perfect when you walk back in.
  2. If it's staying liquidy, it's too boozy — cut it with more colada base or juice to drop the ABV and it'll catch.

7The 15-Minute Final Push

When you're home (~10:15). One short burst and everything lands hot together.

  • Turn the fryer / oil up to 350°F.
  • While it heats: bhindi pan on high — re-crisp 4–5 min, add ¾ tsp salt + ¾ tsp amchur, then off heat finish with ¾ tsp garam masala, juice of ½ lemon and 2 tbsp cilantro.
  • Rice tadka: 2 tbsp ghee + 1 tsp cumin, then ½ tsp garam masala + ¼ tsp black pepper — pour over and fluff.
  • When the oil hits 350°F: second-fry the par-fried samosas & kachori 2–3 min, drain on a rack.
  • Pour the cowboy colada. Plate everything with tamarind-mint chutney or yogurt. Eat.
