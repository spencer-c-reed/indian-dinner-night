Cook Order

Every step from all six components, interleaved in the exact order you do them. The color stripe and tag tell you which dish each step belongs to.

Prep Dough Samosa Kachori Rice Bhindi Slushi Finish
NowSetup

Do these the moment you start — the okra needs hours to dry.

  1. Bhindi
    Wash 1 lb okra and spread it on a towel to dry completely (1–2 hrs). Bone-dry okra is the only way to avoid slime. Don't slice yet.
  2. Prep
    Grab fresh cilantro if you haven't (and a few fresh green chilies if you want them). Pull the chilled boiled potatoes out so they're handy.
~3:00Dough

Make it first so it can rest while you do everything else.

  1. Dough
    D1Stir together 12 cups flour, 4½ tsp salt and 3½ tsp ajwain.
  2. Dough
    D2Rub in 24 tbsp ghee 3–4 min until it looks like coarse sand and clumps when squeezed.
  3. Dough
    D3Add ~3¼ cups cold water gradually into a firm, stiff, non-sticky dough; knead 4 min. Cover and rest 45–60 min.
~3:15While the dough rests

Roast spices and cook both fillings now. Both must cool completely before you fill.

  1. Samosa
    S1Dry-roast 4 tbsp coriander seeds, 3 tsp fennel and 3 tsp cumin ~3 min; coarsely crush. Separately roast & grind 2 tsp cumin as bhuna jeera — set aside. (Last 2 tsp cumin is for tempering.)
  2. Samosa
    S2Crumble the chilled potatoes into ½" chunks. Mince ¼ cup ginger; chop 6 green chilies and 2 onions; crush ½ cup cashews; chop 1 cup cilantro.
  3. Samosa
    S3Heat 6 tbsp ghee; 2 tsp cumin 30 sec, then ¼ tsp hing + ginger + chilies 1 min. Onions 6–8 min. Lower heat; 2 tsp turmeric, 2 tsp Kashmiri + 1 tsp hot (lal mirch) chili + crushed seed blend, 60 sec.
  4. Samosa
    S4Fold in potatoes, ½ cup cashews, ~2½ tsp salt, ¾ tsp black salt, 4 min. Add 1 tbsp amchur, 2 tsp garam masala, 1 tbsp kasuri methi, 1–2 min. Off heat: 1 cup cilantro, the bhuna jeera, juice of 1 lemon. Spread to cool.
  5. Kachori
    K1Thaw and coarsely crush 2 cups green peas (textured, not paste).
  6. Kachori
    K2Heat 2 tbsp ghee; 1 tsp cumin + 1 pinch hing 30 sec; 1 tbsp ginger + 2 minced chilies 1 min. Add 2 tbsp besan, toast 2 min until nutty.
  7. Kachori
    K3Add the peas, 1 tsp coriander, 1 tsp ground fennel, ½ tsp cumin, ½ tsp Kashmiri, ¾ tsp salt; cook 8–10 min until dry. Off heat: ¾ tsp amchur, ½ tsp garam masala, ¼ tsp sugar, 2 tbsp cilantro. Cool completely.
  8. Samosa
    S5Mix the seal paste: 3 tbsp flour + 3 tbsp water into a thick paste.
~4:30Shape

The long stretch. Fillings must be fully cool first.

  1. Dough
    D4Knead the rested dough briefly; split ¾ for samosas, ¼ for kachori.
  2. Samosa
    S6Roll a ball to a 6–7" oval, cut in half. Brush the edge with paste, fold a cone, fill 2–3 tbsp, seal with no gaps. Keep tonight's tray out; freeze the rest shaped & raw.
  3. Kachori
    K4Divide into ~15 balls; cup each into a 3" disc, fill ~1 tbsp, pleat closed, flatten to a 2½–3" disc. Keep a few for tonight; refrigerate/freeze the rest.
~7:30Slow fry & pre-cooks

Get everything to a held state. Nothing here is finished — that's deliberate.

  1. Samosa
    S7Heat oil to 275°F. Slow-fry tonight's samosas 18–22 min — barely bubbling, shell pale and blistered. Cool on a rack. Do not finish.
  2. Kachori
    K5In the same 275°F oil, slow-fry a few kachori 12–15 min until pale and puffed. Cool on the rack alongside the samosas.
  3. Rice
    R1~7:45 — Rinse 1½ cups basmati; into the cooker with 2¼ cups water, 1 tsp salt, 1 bay leaf. Start it, leave on warm. (Tadka comes later.)
  4. Bhindi
    B1~8:00 — Slice the dried okra into ½" rounds. Heat 3 tbsp oil; 1 tsp cumin + pinch hing 30 sec. Okra in a single layer — don't stir 3–4 min, then uncovered 12–15 min until browned. Add 1 sliced onion + 2 chilies 3–4 min; ½ tsp turmeric, 1 tsp coriander, ¾ tsp Kashmiri 60 sec. Stop here, slightly underdone — hold the salt, amchur and finish.
  5. Slushi
    SL1~8:30 (or earlier) — Load the cowboy colada and start the Ninja Slushi. It holds slush for hours, so an early start is fine and gives it room to catch.
8:45Airport
Leave for JFK. Oil off and covered, everything cooling or holding. ~25 min free-flow / ~11 mi — budget 35–40 with the curbside wait. Pickup ~9:30, home ~10:15.
~10:15Final push · ~15 min

One short burst. Everything lands hot together.

  1. Finish
    F1Turn the fryer / oil up to 350°F.
  2. Bhindi
    B2While the oil heats: bhindi pan on high, re-crisp 4–5 min. Add ¾ tsp salt + ¾ tsp amchur; off heat finish with ¾ tsp garam masala, juice of ½ lemon, 2 tbsp cilantro.
  3. Rice
    R2Rice tadka: 2 tbsp ghee + 1 tsp cumin, then off heat ½ tsp garam masala + ¼ tsp black pepper. Pour over, pull the bay leaf, fluff, salt to taste.
  4. Samosa
    S8When the oil hits 350°F: second-fry the par-fried samosas 2–3 min to deep golden, drain on a rack.
  5. Kachori
    K6Finish the kachori in the same 350°F pass, 2–3 min, drain.
  6. Slushi
    SL2Pour the cowboy colada.
  7. Finish
    F2Plate everything with tamarind-mint chutney or yogurt. Eat.
